--- layout: post title: My first hour with the OpenJDK... date: '2015-01-17T02:52:00.000-08:00' author: Christopher Batey tags: modified_time: '2015-01-17T02:52:54.177-08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-4161315644722406995.post-4464327876943879961 blogger_orig_url: https://www.blogger.com/comment.g?blogID=4161315644722406995&postID=4464327876943879961 --- Yesterday I decided that I'd like to give back to the OpenJDK, Mani and Daniel from the LJC gave a lightning talk at the latest LJC Open Conference and they have inspired me!

So here's my first hour trying to work out how to get involved..

So my first google for me to the OpenJDK website: http://openjdk.java.net/guide/repositories.html

I'm on a mac and have I've never used mercurial so had to install it, brew to the rescue!

I then followed the guide for installing the trees extension for mercurial: http://openjdk.java.net/projects/code-tools/trees/

This allowed me to check the whole jdk 9 development branch out:

hg tclone http://hg.openjdk.java.net/jdk9/dev 9dev

I am currently visiting family in the country with very slow internet so this checkout took about 1 hour (I didn't include that in my first hour with the OpenJDK).

Then from the following page I learn the nifty commands for status, checking incoming changes and checking any outgoing changes: http://openjdk.java.net/projects/code-tools/trees/

So now I have the Open JDK checked out, I quickly found a very useful file called: README-builds.html I won't replicate any of that here as it will soon get out of date.
